# PacketHero - Vision

## High Concept

PacketHero is an adventure and learning game focused on networking.

The player controls a curious 12-year-old child with no prior knowledge of computers or networking.

One day, while trying to help his mother restore a broken Internet connection, he touches a malfunctioning router and experiences a strange electrical shock.

After this event, he gains the ability to see networks, cables, packets and data flows that are normally invisible.

By helping people around him solve technical problems, he progressively discovers the concepts behind modern computer networks.

The goal is not to teach CCNA directly.

The goal is to make players understand networking naturally through exploration, storytelling and troubleshooting.

---

## Learning Philosophy

PacketHero is not a CCNA course.

PacketHero is a game that teaches networking concepts.

Players learn because they need knowledge to solve problems.

Theory comes after curiosity and necessity.

---

## Main Progression

The player progresses through increasingly complex environments.

Example progression:

* Home Network
* Small Office
* Enterprise Network
* City Network
* Internet Backbone

Each area introduces new concepts and new challenges.

---

## Side Quests

Optional missions reinforce learning.

Examples:

* Grandfather cannot print.
* Girlfriend thinks she has been hacked.
* Neighbor bought a new Wi-Fi router.
* School computers cannot connect to the network.

Side quests provide additional XP and deeper understanding.

---

## Troubleshooting Focus

PacketHero is fundamentally a troubleshooting game.

Players investigate problems, gather clues and apply networking concepts to find solutions.

---

## The OSI Powers

The hero's special abilities are visual representations of the OSI model.

Each important milestone unlocks a new way to perceive the network.

### Layer 1 - Physical Vision

The player can see:

* Cables
* Physical connections
* Broken links

### Layer 2 - Frame Vision

The player can see:

* Frames
* MAC addresses
* Local switching activity

### Layer 3 - Packet Vision

The player can see:

* IP addresses
* Packets
* Routes

### Layer 4 - Transport Vision

The player can see:

* TCP
* UDP
* Ports

### Layer 5 - Session Vision

The player can see:

* Sessions
* Active connections
* Connection states

### Layer 6 - Presentation Vision

The player can see:

* Encryption
* Compression
* Encodings

### Layer 7 - Application Vision

The player can see:

* Services
* DNS names
* Applications

---

## Main Story Twist

At a major point in the story, the hero temporarily loses his powers.

The player must solve network problems without visual assistance.

This transforms learning into mastery.

The player no longer relies on powers.

The player relies on knowledge.

---

## End Goal

The player starts as a child who knows nothing about networking.

The player finishes as someone capable of understanding and troubleshooting complex network environments through acquired knowledge and experience.
